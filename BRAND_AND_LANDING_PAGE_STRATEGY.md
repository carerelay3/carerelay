# CareRelay Brand And Landing Page Strategy

Date: 2026-05-13

## Does CareRelay Work Beyond Caregiving?

CareRelay sounds care-oriented, but not so narrow that it cannot support family coordination. “Relay” is broad: passing updates among people. “Care” can mean caregiving, family care, household care, and team care, but it will feel less natural for roommates, frats, sports teams, and group trips.

Recommendation: keep CareRelay caregiver-first now. Use sub-positioning for expansion:

- CareRelay for Caregiving.
- CareRelay Family.
- CareRelay Household.
- CareRelay Teams.

Do not rename the main brand until Family Mode demand is proven.

## Homepage Strategy

The homepage should remain caregiver-specific through beta. It is the clearest buyer pain and safest product fit. Add expansion landing pages only when the product can create matching circle types or when running demand tests with waitlists.

## Proposed Landing Pages

### `/caregivers`

- Headline: One shared number for family caregiving updates.
- Subheadline: Turn texts about tasks, appointments, supplies, medication confirmations, concerns, and daily summaries into one calm dashboard.
- Primary CTA: Try the caregiving demo.
- Target customer: adult children, spouses, siblings caring for a loved one.
- Feature cards: Shared SMS line, Medication confirmations, Care tasks, Concern timeline, Daily summaries.
- Disclaimer needs: full medical boundary and emergency disclaimer.

### `/families`

- Headline: A family command center that starts with a text.
- Subheadline: Coordinate chores, errands, appointments, groceries, school notes, reminders, and family updates without making everyone install another app.
- Primary CTA: Join Family Mode beta.
- Target customer: busy households, co-parents, extended families.
- Feature cards: Chores and errands, Groceries, Appointments, School notes, Family summaries.
- Disclaimer needs: general coordination disclaimer; medical disclaimer only if health/care examples appear.

### `/households`

- Headline: Keep the house on the same page by text.
- Subheadline: Roommates can text chores, groceries, bills, maintenance, events, and house reminders into one shared dashboard.
- Primary CTA: Join Household Mode waitlist.
- Target customer: roommates, dorm suites, shared houses.
- Feature cards: Chore rotation, Groceries and supplies, Bills/reminders, Maintenance log, House announcements.
- Disclaimer needs: consent, privacy, opt-out, no safety monitoring.

### `/teams`

- Headline: Team updates that parents and players actually see.
- Subheadline: Coaches and team managers can coordinate practices, games, rides, equipment, volunteers, and announcements through SMS.
- Primary CTA: Join Team Mode waitlist.
- Target customer: youth sports coaches, team parents, clubs.
- Feature cards: Schedule changes, Ride coordination, Equipment checklist, Volunteer tasks, Parent announcements.
- Disclaimer needs: minors’ data, consent, opt-out, no emergency/weather safety guarantee.

### `/groups`

- Headline: One text-friendly command center for group plans.
- Subheadline: Coordinate events, tasks, supplies, announcements, decisions, and reminders for trips, clubs, and friend groups.
- Primary CTA: Join Group Mode waitlist.
- Target customer: trip organizers, club officers, group planners.
- Feature cards: Events, Tasks, Supplies, Decisions, Summaries.
- Disclaimer needs: consent, admin controls, member removal, abuse reporting.

## Branding Guardrails

- Keep medical boundary visible anywhere caregiving, medication, concerns, or summaries are mentioned.
- Avoid “monitoring,” “alerts,” “safety system,” “keeps them safe,” or “never miss.”
- Use “family-reported,” “coordination,” “organization,” “shared notes,” and “for review.”
- Use “SMS-based” carefully: do not imply device SMS inbox access.
- For minors/team pages, mention parent/guardian consent and opt-out.
